### Live pricing research - reviewed 2026-07-01

Sage does **not** publish fixed Sage Intacct plan names, list prices, usage tiers, or overage schedules on its U.S. pricing page. The official page describes Sage Intacct pricing as a custom accounting software pricing plan tailored by industry, organization size, and selected modules, and directs buyers to request pricing rather than self-serve purchase. See Sage's [Sage Intacct pricing page](https://www.sage.com/en-us/sage-business-cloud/intacct/pricing/?referral=altavista).

| Pricing item | Public status | Buyer-useful notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Public plan names | Not publicly disclosed | Sage says each solution is configured from combinations of modules and advanced functionality; no named Good/Better/Best or seat-tier plan table was found. |
| Public prices | Not publicly disclosed | No monthly or annual list price is published; buyers need a Sage or reseller quote. |
| Price basis | Publicly described as module-dependent | Sage states pricing is based on included modules and the organization's needs. |
| Billing thresholds / usage limits | Not publicly disclosed | No public thresholds for entities, transactions, API calls, contracts, invoices, storage, or employees were found. |
| Overage rules | Not publicly disclosed | The public Terms cover add-on subscriptions and additional licenses/volume, but not a general overage schedule. |
| Add-ons / modules | Public module inventory exists | Sage's application subscriptions page lists modules such as Accounts Payable, Accounts Receivable, Advanced Audit Trail, Advanced CRM Integration, Avalara Tax, Consolidation, Contracts, Fixed Assets, Inventory Control, Multi-Entity Management, Order Entry, Projects, Purchasing, Revenue Management, and Planning connector-related subscriptions. |
| Contract / renewal mechanics | Publicly disclosed in U.S. Terms | Services are purchased as time-based subscriptions unless an order says otherwise; user subscriptions are named-user subscriptions; subscriptions auto-renew for one-year periods unless notice of non-renewal is given at least 30 days before period end. |
| Mid-term additions | Publicly disclosed in U.S. Terms | Additional volume/licenses for existing subscribed items are billed prorata at the underlying pre-existing subscription price; new subscription items are prorated at then-current list price unless the order says otherwise. |
| Fee changes | Publicly disclosed in U.S. Terms | Sage says it gives at least 30 days' notice before applying fee changes; unless the order says otherwise, changes do not affect prices during the then-current subscription term and apply at renewal. |
| Taxes / late fees | Publicly disclosed in U.S. Terms | Fees are exclusive of taxes; late fees accrue interest at the lower of 1.5% per month or the maximum permitted by law. |
| Implementation / training | Requires quote / SOW | Sage's Terms state professional services such as implementation, training, or consulting are outside the Services and require a statement of work or separate written agreement. |

#### Buyer caveats to verify in the quote

- Confirm the licensed legal entities, currencies, users by role, transaction/invoice/contract volumes, API or Web Services license, sandbox/training tenants, and whether subscription-billing or revenue-management modules are included.
- Ask Sage or the reseller to itemize all add-ons: Contracts, Revenue Management, Advanced Audit Trail, Advanced CRM/Salesforce integration, Avalara Tax, Planning, AI/ML features, AP automation, support tier, implementation, migration, and marketplace/partner connector fees.
- Request renewal price protections, discount expiration dates, minimum term, non-renewal notice workflow, and whether module removal affects bundled discounts.
- For integrations, note that Sage's Terms say third-party services are not warranted or guaranteed for ongoing interoperability by Sage unless separately agreed.

## News details

### square-point-of-sale-2026-06-25-square-ships-pos-improvements-for-alerts-variation-controls-audio-cues-and-item-grids

Square's [feature log](https://squareup.com/us/en/feature-log) lists POS improvements around alerts, variation controls, audio cues, and item grids.

This is not subscription billing in the narrow sense, but it is useful buyer context for businesses comparing commerce operations. POS updates affect speed at the counter, staff workflow, online-order awareness, and how quickly sellers can find or configure items during a rush.

- Buyer signal: Square keeps investing in day-to-day seller workflow, which can matter more to retailers and restaurants than back-office billing depth.
- Follow-up: Evaluate whether the improvements apply to the buyer's Square plan, hardware, industry mode, and staff permissions model.

### square-point-of-sale-2025-05-13-square-launches-square-handheld-for-pocketable-pos-operations

Square launched [Square Handheld](https://squareup.com/us/en/press/square-handheld), a pocketable POS device aimed at mobile checkout and floor operations.

For restaurants, retailers, and service businesses, hardware can change the software evaluation. A mobile device can support line busting, table-side ordering, curbside workflows, or sales-floor checkout without forcing staff back to a fixed terminal.

- Buyer signal: Square's POS story includes first-party hardware, which can reduce integration risk for sellers that want a packaged commerce stack.
- Follow-up: Compare battery life, payment methods, offline behavior, durability, accessories, device management, and compatibility with the buyer's industry workflows.

### square-point-of-sale-2025-04-24-square-introduces-unified-point-of-sale-app-with-seven-industry-modes

Square introduced a unified Point of Sale app with seven industry modes in an [official Square release](https://squareup.com/us/en/press/single-point-of-sale).

The product strategy is consolidation. Instead of forcing sellers to reason across separate POS apps, Square is trying to route restaurant, retail, services, bookings, bar, quick-service, and standard POS workflows through one interface with mode-specific behavior.

- Buyer signal: Useful for sellers that operate across formats or expect their business model to evolve.
- Follow-up: Test whether the unified app preserves the depth of specialized workflows, especially inventory, modifiers, service bookings, staff permissions, and reporting.

### Vendor-attested claim candidates - reviewed 2026-07-01

The following are **claim candidates from PayPal-controlled public sources**. They should not be rewritten as neutral G2 findings without independent validation or buyer testing.

| Claim candidate | Vendor source | OKF treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "No fee to create or send an invoice"; processing fees apply when customers pay. | [PayPal Invoicing](https://www.paypal.com/us/business/accept-payments/invoice?gh_jid=4602865) and PayPal help/pricing pages | Suitable as a vendor-attested pricing claim with fee-page caveats. |
| "77% of PayPal invoices are paid within one day of sending." | PayPal Invoicing page footnote says this is based on PayPal internal data from Aug. 1, 2023 to July 31, 2024. | Treat as internal-data marketing claim; do not generalize to a buyer's expected DSO or payment speed. |
| "755M+ PayPal invoices sent globally" and "$144B paid globally through PayPal invoices." | PayPal Invoicing page footnote says based on internal data from launch in 2010 through February 2025. | Treat as scale claim; not evidence of current product fit or customer satisfaction. |
| Invoice payment options include PayPal, Venmo, Apple Pay, cards, Pay Later, and Pay by Bank (ACH). | PayPal Invoicing page and U.S. fee page | Treat as vendor-attested availability; confirm by merchant country, payer country, and account setup. |
| PayPal Invoicing is available in over 200 markets and 24 currencies. | PayPal Invoicing FAQ section | Treat as vendor-attested geographic/currency claim; verify country-specific product and fee availability. |
| PayPal says it is fully PCI compliant and provides downloadable audit reports including PCI AoC, SOC1/2, and ISO 27001 certificate through its portal. | PayPal network-compliance and compliance-report FAQ pages | Treat as broad PayPal compliance claim; buyers should obtain reports and confirm PayPal Invoicing scope. |

#### Claims requiring vendor confirmation before buyer reliance

- Exact feature scope of the **Invoice Subscription Service** listed at $14.99/month.
- Whether QuickBooks/Xero sync supports the buyer's required entities, tax fields, classes/projects, multi-currency handling, and reconciliation workflow.
- Current API limits, uptime commitments, support SLAs, and incident-notification process for PayPal Invoicing.
- Whether SOC2/ISO/PCI report scope explicitly covers PayPal Invoicing and the buyer's intended integration path.
